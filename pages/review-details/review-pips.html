Shimpoo Pimpoo

PIPSCHIPS&VIDEOCLIPS

Shimpoo Pimpoo operates less as a polished album and more like a chaotic, undeniably vibrant scrapbook of Zagreb. At its core, the record captures a band that wasn't content picking a lane; Pips, Chips & Videoclips seem undecided on whether they wanted to be football hooligans, psychedelic rockers, or cabaret stars, so they audaciously attempted to be everything at once. While this results in a total lack of focus, there is a distinct, scruffy charm to the confusion. Ripper isn't singing so much as he is hooting and hollering, perfectly channeling a specific brand of post-war urban cynicism—the sound of trying to keep your cool while the world around you is in flux.

Still, for a debut record, Pips did a pretty good job of potraying of the chaotic early 90s of Croatia, and would later develop as a band with records like Dernjava and Fred Astaire, although never surpassing the popularity of "Dinamo ja volim".

Released: December 1995.
