Balegari ne vjeruju sreći

Štulić, Azra

This is the sound of Johnny Štulić giving up on the idea of a "band" (while still working on Balegari with old bandmates, somehow) and just letting his own eccentricity run the show. Unlike the tight, nervous energy of early Azra, Balegari is a sprawling, murky tavern brawl of a record. He dives headfirst into sevdah and Balkan folk, treating "Usne vrele višnje" not as a cover but as a reinvention of local sorrow.

The flaw here is undeniable, though: the production is garbage. The drum machines sound cheap and the mix is muddy. It sounds like it was recorded on a cassette player in a damp basement. But weirdly, that incompetence works in its favor—it feels less like a product and more like a drunken confession you overhear at 3 AM.

Released: 1990.

Contributed: Branimir Štulić (vocals/guitar),
Branko Knežević (drums), Jurica Pađen (guitar), Tomislav Šojat (bass),
Theodore Yanni (engineer) and Branimir Štulić (producer).
