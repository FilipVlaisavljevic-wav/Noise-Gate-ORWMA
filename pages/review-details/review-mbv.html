Isn't Anything

My Bloody Valentine

Before they dissolved into the warm, fuzzy bliss of Loveless, My Bloody Valentine were actually kind of scary. Isn't Anything is the nastier, sharper sibling. It sounds like the band is physically wrestling their instruments. The "glide guitar" technique is there, but it converts into much different textures than the future MBV sound. There’s a sleazy, uncomfortable yet striking disonance to tracks like "Sueisfine" that they later scrubbed away. The weak point is that the songwriting often takes a backseat to the texture, which can be subpar at times. A few tracks feel like half-finished sketches where they figured out a cool noise but forgot to write a chorus. It’s a groundbreaking record and a setting stone to Loveless, but it’s also jagged and occasionally irritating in a way they never were again.

Released: November 1, 1988.
