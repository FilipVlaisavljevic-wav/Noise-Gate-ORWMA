Timelapse

Kinokoteikoku

Kinoko Teikoku (eng. Mushroom Kingdom) alienated a lot of their shoegaze purist fans with this one, and you can see why. They traded the wall of noise for clean, delayed guitars and whisper-close vocals influenced by J-Rock. The production is pristine, capturing the feeling of watching city lights blur through a taxi window. It’s melancholic and incredibly pretty.

But the criticism that it’s "too safe" isn't wrong. By smoothing out all the rough edges, the album risks becoming background wallpaper. It lacks the explosive catharsis of their earlier work like Eureka. It’s a beautiful mood piece, but sometimes you find yourself waiting for a climax that never actually arrives.

Released: November 4, 2015.

Contributed: Chiaki Sato (vocals/guitar),
Shigeaki Nagaya (guitar), Kousuke Nishimura (bass) and Tatsuru Konno (drums).
