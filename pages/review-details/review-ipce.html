Činio sam čuda

Ipče Ahmedovski

Činio sam čuda isn’t just a folk record; it is the sonic equivalent of driving a Mercedes 190D off a cliff while sobbing. Ipče Ahmedovski, the "Little Rambo," captured the essence of dance folk music perfectly with his vocals. Činio sam čuda captures the moment Yugoslav folk music stopped being rural and started becoming urban, chaotic, and dangerous with Novica Urošević's lyrics.

The concept here is "hysteria as an art form." Ipče’s voice—that piercing, high-pitched tenor that sounds like it’s constantly on the verge of cracking—is the main instrument. In the title track, he isn't singing about heartbreak; he is screaming an indictment against fate itself. It’s the soundtrack for people who have lost everything at roulette and found themselves in the local bar ordering round after round with no money for the check.

Released: 1990.

Contributed: Novica Urošević (lyrics), Orkestar Novice Nikolića (music)
and Ipče Ahmedovski (vocals)
